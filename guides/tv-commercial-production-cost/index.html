TV Commercial Production Cost in NYC: Complete 2026 Guide

Transparent breakdown of what you'll actually pay for TV commercial production in New York City, from concept to final delivery.

Last updated: January 19, 2026 | 12 min read

Quick Cost Summary

  • Simple Production: $15,000 - $35,000
  • Mid-Range Production: $35,000 - $75,000
  • High-End Production: $75,000 - $150,000+
  • Average Timeline: 4-8 weeks

Understanding TV Commercial Production Costs

TV commercial production costs vary widely based on creative complexity, talent, locations, production days, and post-production requirements. This guide breaks down every cost component so you can budget accurately.

Phase 1: Pre-Production Costs ($5,000 - $20,000)

Pre-production is the planning phase where your commercial takes shape creatively and logistically.

Creative Development ($2,000 - $8,000)

  • Scriptwriting: $1,000 - $3,000 for professional copywriting
  • Storyboarding: $1,000 - $3,000 for detailed visual planning
  • Concept development: $500 - $2,000 for initial ideation

Casting ($1,000 - $5,000)

  • Casting director fee: $500 - $2,000
  • Audition space rental: $200 - $500 per day
  • Casting platform fees: $300 - $800

Location Scouting & Permits ($1,000 - $5,000)

  • Location scout fee: $500 - $2,000
  • NYC filming permits: $300 - $1,500
  • Location fees: Varies widely (free to $5,000+ per day)

Producer/Production Manager ($1,000 - $2,000)

Essential for coordinating all pre-production activities and keeping the project on track.

Phase 2: Production Costs ($8,000 - $80,000)

The filming phase is typically the most expensive, especially in NYC where crew and location costs are higher.

Crew ($3,000 - $15,000 per day)

  • Director: $1,000 - $5,000 per day
  • Director of Photography: $800 - $3,000 per day
  • Gaffer (lighting): $500 - $1,200 per day
  • Key Grip: $500 - $1,000 per day
  • Sound recordist: $500 - $1,000 per day
  • Production assistants: $200 - $400 per day each

Talent ($1,000 - $30,000)

  • Non-union talent: $500 - $2,000 per day + buyout
  • SAG-AFTRA union talent: $1,000 - $10,000 per day + residuals
  • Celebrity endorsers: $10,000 - $100,000+
  • Usage rights/buyouts: $500 - $20,000 (varies by distribution)

Equipment ($2,000 - $10,000 per day)

  • Camera package: $500 - $3,000 per day
  • Lighting: $500 - $3,000 per day
  • Grip equipment: $300 - $1,500 per day
  • Sound equipment: $200 - $800 per day

Production Support ($1,000 - $5,000 per day)

  • Craft services & catering: $300 - $1,000 per day
  • Transportation & parking: $200 - $1,000 per day
  • Insurance: $500 - $2,000 per project
  • Wardrobe & props: $500 - $3,000

Phase 3: Post-Production Costs ($5,000 - $30,000)

Post-production brings your commercial to life through editing, color grading, sound design, and finishing.

Editing ($2,000 - $10,000)

  • Editor fee: $500 - $2,000 per day (5-10 days typical)
  • Editing suite: Often included in editor rate
  • Multiple versions: $500 - $2,000 per additional cut

Color Grading ($1,000 - $5,000)

  • Colorist: $500 - $1,500 per day (1-3 days)
  • Color suite: Often included

Motion Graphics & VFX ($1,000 - $10,000)

  • Simple graphics: $1,000 - $3,000
  • Complex animation: $3,000 - $10,000
  • VFX (if needed): $5,000 - $50,000+

Sound Design & Mixing ($1,000 - $5,000)

  • Sound designer: $500 - $1,500
  • Final mix: $500 - $2,000
  • Voiceover talent: $500 - $3,000
  • Music licensing: $500 - $5,000 (varies widely)

Real-World Examples

Example 1: Simple Testimonial Commercial ($18,000)

  • 1 day shoot in office location
  • 3 customer interviews
  • Small crew (4 people)
  • Basic lighting setup
  • 1-week post-production
  • Deliverables: :30 and :15 versions

Example 2: Mid-Range Product Commercial ($55,000)

  • 2 days shoot (studio + lifestyle location)
  • Professional actors (2 principals)
  • Medium crew (8-10 people)
  • Professional lighting & camera package
  • Motion graphics & VFX
  • 2-week post-production
  • Licensed music

Example 3: High-End Brand Campaign ($125,000)

  • 3 days shoot (multiple locations)
  • SAG-AFTRA talent (multiple actors)
  • Large crew (15+ people)
  • Premium equipment (RED camera, specialized rigs)
  • Complex lighting setups
  • Extensive motion graphics
  • 3-week post-production
  • Original music composition

How to Reduce Costs Without Sacrificing Quality

  1. Simplify locations: Use one location instead of multiple
  2. Limit shoot days: Tight pre-production planning maximizes efficiency
  3. Use non-union talent: When appropriate for your distribution
  4. Leverage stock assets: Music and b-roll can be cost-effective
  5. Bundle services: Work with one agency vs. multiple vendors
  6. Optimize for reuse: Shoot content that can be repurposed

NYC-Specific Cost Considerations

Filming in New York City comes with unique cost factors:

  • Higher crew rates: NYC crews typically charge 20-30% more than other markets
  • Permit requirements: Most outdoor filming requires city permits
  • Parking & logistics: Can add $500-$1,500 per day
  • Union considerations: NYC is a strong union market
  • Location fees: Prime NYC locations command premium rates

Questions to Ask Before Committing

  1. What's included in the quote? (Be specific about deliverables)
  2. How many revision rounds are included?
  3. What are the payment terms?
  4. Who owns the raw footage?
  5. What usage rights are included?
  6. What happens if we go over budget?
  7. What's the cancellation policy?
